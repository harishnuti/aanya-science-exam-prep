How do humans reproduce?

Before we find out how humans reproduce, let us understand why living things need to reproduce.
If living things do not reproduce, there will be fewer and fewer of them (endangered) and they will eventually all die out (extinct). Once a living thing becomes extinct, we will no longer see its kind. Hence, living things reproduce so that their kind will always be around.

Humans can only reproduce after they have reached puberty. Puberty is a natural part of growing up. During puberty, changes happen inside the body. For example, males start to produce millions of sperms in their testes, while eggs in females begin to mature.

What are the states of matter that water can exist in?

Matter can exist in different states, such as solid, liquid and gas. One example of matter that is readily available to us in all three states is water. We can find water in the solid, liquid and gaseous states around us.
Water in the solid state is known as ice.

Let's Connect

Ice can be made in the freezer at home.

In some countries, snow may fall during winter. Snow is made of tiny pieces of ice.
Large pieces of ice can be found floating in the water in very cold places, such as Antarctica. These large pieces of ice are known as icebergs.

Observation: Ice melts when it is taken out of a freezer and placed on a table.

What you have learnt: Heat flows from a place of higher temperature to a place of lower temperature.

Explanation for the melting of ice: The temperature of ice is lower than the temperature of the surroundings. Therefore, ice will gain heat from its surroundings. The heat gained by ice causes it to melt at 0°C.

Science Around Us
Animals such as polar bears need ice caps to survive. However, due to increasing human activities such as the increased use of transportation and electronic devices, more fossil fuels are burnt to produce more energy. With more fossil fuels burnt, more greenhouse gases are produced. Greenhouse gases trap heat and cause a rise in the Earth's temperature. This causes the ice in the Arctic and Antarctica to melt faster. With fewer ice caps due to melting, the polar bears' survival is threatened.

Ice caps in the Arctic

We can do our part to slow down the rise in the Earth's temperature. For example, we can take public transport and turn off electronic devices when we are not using them.

When a bulb in an electric circuit lights up, we say that current flows through it. This happens when both terminals of a battery are connected correctly to the metal parts of the bulb.

A closed circuit
