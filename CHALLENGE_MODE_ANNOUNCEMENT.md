## 📚 Sample Challenge Questions

### Example 1: Water Cycles (Tricky!)
**Question**: "During evaporation from the ocean, salt crystals form on the water surface. Why does this NOT happen?"

**Options** (All seem logical at first!):
- A) Salt molecules are too heavy to evaporate
- B) Salt water has a lower boiling point than fresh water
- C) **Only water molecules have enough energy to escape as vapor** ← CORRECT
- D) Salt dissolves more in hot water and cannot crystallize

**Why it's tricky**: Students think salt molecules can evaporate because they see salt in seawater. But salt is NON-VOLATILE - only water molecules can escape as vapor! This is the KEY concept.

---

### Example 2: Electrical Systems (Confusing!)
**Question**: "In a series circuit with 2 bulbs, adding another identical bulb makes the circuit dimmer. What is the MAIN reason?"

**Options** (Sound equally plausible!):
- A) The battery voltage decreases when more bulbs are added
- B) **Adding a bulb increases total resistance, so current decreases** ← CORRECT
- C) The electrons move slower through more bulbs
- D) The battery loses power faster with more bulbs

**Why it's tricky**: Options A, B, C all seem like valid physics! But only B is the correct application of Ohm's Law (I = V/R). The battery voltage actually STAYS the same!

---

### Example 3: Reproduction (Subtle!)
**Question**: "Identical twins have 100% same DNA, fraternal twins share ~50%. If both sets are raised in the SAME environment, which will be more alike?"

**Options** (Environment vs Genetics!):
- A) Fraternal twins (same environment overrides DNA)
- B) **Identical twins (same DNA makes them look identical)** ← CORRECT
- C) Both pairs will be equally alike (environment matters more)
- D) It depends on the parents' genes

**Why it's tricky**: Nature vs Nurture question! Students might think environment matters most, but genetics STILL dominates for appearance!

## 🎯 Design Philosophy

### Why These Questions Are Effective

✅ **Multi-concept combinations**
- Not just testing recall
- Requires understanding relationships between concepts

✅ **Similar-looking distractors**
- All options are plausible initially
- Must read carefully to distinguish
- Tests deep comprehension, not guessing

✅ **Common misconceptions as traps**
- Wrong answers appeal to natural (but incorrect) thinking
- Teaches what NOT to think
- Explains WHY certain answers are wrong

✅ **Scenario-based**
- Real-world applications
- Tests whether student can apply knowledge
- More challenging than simple definition questions

✅ **PSLE-authentic**
- Based on actual PSLE examination patterns
- Trains thinking style for real exam
- Builds exam confidence
